Today's View: Branding

Some of you might have noticed over the years certain changes to things that you've bought. Cereal mascots, product packaging images, and even design changes for logos, websites, and applications. Doing this is fairly commonplace among all businesses who wish to remain relevant and up-to-date during their time as an active business. After all, branding is just another form of marketing. However, there are certain instances in which branding can be negatively used. For example, there are several companies that will actively alter their logos and their brand image once a year during what is now known as "Pride Month". While it is perceived as a visual representation of their support, I don't believe it is so. In fact, I believe that those companies are just trying to remain relevant to the LGBTQIA+ community so they do not take their support away from them. The loss of those users/supporters would cost these companies millions of dollars.

So, if I were a business and I didn't want to lose an entire communitiy's support- or money, I should say-, what should I do? Alter my marketing strategy to appeal to that community and keep earning that money. I don't believe that a majority of these multi-million dollar companies care about the communities they claim to support. Instead, I do believe they want to keep that chunk of revenue coming in. Now, I'm not saying that all companies do this. I'm saying that there are some companies that will claim to support a movement, culture, or community but won't do anything significant to actually show support other than switching up their marketing strategy for a time.

Needless to say, despite companies not actually supporting what they claim to, there are other companies that actually do support what and whom they claim. A prime example is this: Google. Google has pioneered LGBTQ+ healthcare by including it into their healthcare policies. That's just one way a company can actually support their supporters.
